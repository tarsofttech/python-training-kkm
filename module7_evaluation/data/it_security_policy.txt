INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY SECURITY POLICY
Effective Date: January 1, 2024

PURPOSE

This IT Security Policy establishes guidelines and requirements for protecting the company's information technology resources, data, and systems. All employees, contractors, and third parties with access to company IT resources must comply with this policy.

1. PASSWORD MANAGEMENT

1.1 Password Requirements
All passwords must meet the following minimum requirements:
- At least 12 characters in length
- Include a combination of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters
- Must not contain dictionary words, personal information, or common patterns
- Must be unique and not reused across multiple systems

1.2 Password Changes
Passwords for critical systems must be changed every 90 days. Users will receive automated reminders 7 days before expiration. Temporary passwords issued by IT must be changed immediately upon first login.

1.3 Password Storage
Passwords must never be written down, shared with others, or stored in unencrypted files. Use of approved password management tools is strongly encouraged. If you suspect your password has been compromised, change it immediately and report the incident to IT Security.

2. ACCESS CONTROL

2.1 User Accounts
Each employee will be assigned a unique user account for accessing company systems. Sharing of user credentials is strictly prohibited. User accounts will be created, modified, or disabled by the IT Department based on authorized requests from management.

2.2 Principle of Least Privilege
Users will be granted the minimum level of access necessary to perform their job functions. Requests for elevated privileges must be approved by both the user's supervisor and IT Security, with justification documented.

2.3 Account Termination
When an employee leaves the company or changes roles, their access rights will be reviewed and adjusted accordingly. All access for terminated employees will be revoked within 24 hours of notification to IT.

3. DATA PROTECTION

3.1 Data Classification
Company data is classified into three categories:
- Public: Information that can be freely shared
- Internal: Information for internal use only
- Confidential: Sensitive information requiring strict protection

Employees must handle data according to its classification level and apply appropriate security controls.

3.2 Data Encryption
All confidential data must be encrypted when stored on portable devices or transmitted over networks. Approved encryption tools and methods must be used. Unencrypted storage of confidential data on personal devices is prohibited.

3.3 Data Backup
Critical business data must be backed up regularly according to IT-defined schedules. Employees are responsible for ensuring their important files are stored in designated network locations that are included in backup procedures.

4. DEVICE SECURITY

4.1 Company Devices
All company-issued devices (laptops, desktops, tablets, smartphones) must be protected with:
- Strong passwords or biometric authentication
- Automatic screen lock after 10 minutes of inactivity
- Full disk encryption
- Up-to-date antivirus and anti-malware software
- Latest security patches and updates

4.2 Personal Devices
Use of personal devices for work purposes (BYOD) is permitted only with prior approval and enrollment in the Mobile Device Management (MDM) system. Personal devices must meet minimum security requirements and allow remote wipe capability.

4.3 Lost or Stolen Devices
Any lost or stolen device containing company data must be reported to IT Security immediately, regardless of the time of day. IT will initiate remote wipe procedures to protect company information.

5. NETWORK SECURITY

5.1 Wi-Fi Usage
Employees must connect to the company's secure Wi-Fi network when in the office. Use of public Wi-Fi networks for accessing company resources is permitted only through the company VPN. Connecting to unsecured networks without VPN protection is prohibited.

5.2 VPN Requirements
Remote access to company systems must be conducted through the approved VPN solution. The VPN must be active whenever accessing company resources from outside the office network. Split tunneling is not permitted.

5.3 Network Monitoring
All network traffic is subject to monitoring for security purposes. Employees should have no expectation of privacy when using company networks. Attempts to bypass security controls or monitoring will result in disciplinary action.

6. EMAIL AND COMMUNICATION SECURITY

6.1 Email Usage
Company email is for business purposes. Limited personal use is acceptable but must not interfere with work duties. All emails are subject to monitoring and retention policies.

6.2 Phishing Awareness
Employees must be vigilant against phishing attempts. Suspicious emails should be reported to IT Security immediately. Never click on links or download attachments from unknown senders. Verify unexpected requests for sensitive information through alternative communication channels.

6.3 Sensitive Information
Confidential information sent via email must be encrypted using approved tools. Large files should be shared through secure file transfer services rather than email attachments.

7. INCIDENT REPORTING

7.1 Security Incidents
Security incidents include:
- Suspected malware infections
- Unauthorized access attempts
- Data breaches or leaks
- Lost or stolen devices
- Suspicious system behavior

All security incidents must be reported to IT Security immediately at security@company.com or extension 4911.

7.2 Response Procedures
Upon discovering a security incident:
1. Do not attempt to fix the issue yourself
2. Disconnect the affected device from the network if safe to do so
3. Contact IT Security immediately
4. Document what happened and preserve evidence
5. Follow instructions from IT Security team

7.3 Investigation Cooperation
Employees must cooperate fully with security incident investigations. This may include providing access to devices, accounts, and communications. Failure to cooperate may result in disciplinary action.

8. ACCEPTABLE USE

8.1 Prohibited Activities
The following activities are strictly prohibited:
- Installing unauthorized software
- Accessing, storing, or distributing illegal or inappropriate content
- Attempting to bypass security controls
- Using company resources for personal business ventures
- Connecting unauthorized devices to the company network
- Sharing confidential information with unauthorized parties

8.2 Social Media
Employees must not disclose confidential company information on social media platforms. When posting about work-related topics, include a disclaimer that views are personal and do not represent the company.

8.3 Software Licensing
Only properly licensed software may be installed on company devices. Pirated or unauthorized software is strictly prohibited. Software installation requests must be submitted to IT for approval.

9. TRAINING AND AWARENESS

9.1 Security Training
All employees must complete security awareness training within 30 days of hire and annually thereafter. Additional training may be required for employees with access to sensitive systems or data.

9.2 Policy Acknowledgment
Employees must acknowledge receipt and understanding of this policy. Continued access to IT resources constitutes acceptance of these terms and agreement to comply.

10. COMPLIANCE AND ENFORCEMENT

Violations of this IT Security Policy may result in:
- Verbal or written warnings
- Suspension of IT access privileges
- Termination of employment
- Legal action for serious violations

The severity of consequences will depend on the nature, frequency, and impact of the violation.

For questions or clarification regarding this policy, contact IT Security at security@company.com.
